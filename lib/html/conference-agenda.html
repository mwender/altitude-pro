CB General Session #2
The Department of Justice's Cybersecurity Initiatives-Investigating and Prosecuting Network Intruders in Today's Cyber Landscape

As the frequency of network intrusions grows, stakeholders must face the unique challenges of confronting intruders from inside and outside an organization. By addressing everything from file exfiltration at small businesses to large-scale data breaches at major corporations, the Department of Justice has been at the forefront of identifying and prosecuting network intruders. Tim Flowers, Trial Attorney at the Department's Computer Crime and Intellectual Property Section (CCIPS), investigates and prosecutes cyber-related cases ranging from trade-secret theft to large-scale hacks. Flowers will discuss relevant provisions of the Cybersecurity Information Sharing Act ("CISA"), how companies can work with law enforcement after experiencing a suspected intrusion, and the Department's use of its prosecutorial powers to bring network intruders to justice. Flowers will also discuss CCIPS's efforts to engage businesses, regardless of their sizes, about communicating suspected intrusions to law enforcement.
